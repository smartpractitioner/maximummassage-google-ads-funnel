What is TMJ and jaw tension massage?

TMJ massage is specialized hands-on work that targets the jaw muscles, the surrounding facial muscles, and the connected neck and shoulder patterns that contribute to TMJ symptoms (clicking, popping, soreness, clenching, tension headaches). It can include external work alone, or, with your consent, gentle intra-oral work to address jaw muscles from inside the mouth. The goal isn’t to fix a structural problem, it’s to release the muscle tension and signal the nervous system to ease up.

Benefits of TMJ and tension-headache massage

  • Less jaw clicking, popping, and aching.

    Releasing the muscles around the joint lets it move more freely and often quiets the noise and soreness.

  • Fewer tension headaches.

    Most tension headaches start in the neck, jaw, or base of the skull. Work those spots, headaches drop.

  • Relief from clenching and grinding patterns.

    Stuck contraction in the jaw lets go, and the body learns it doesn’t have to hold tension there at night.

  • Easier sleep when you’re not waking up in tension.

    Lower nighttime tension means deeper sleep and less morning soreness.

  • Better range of motion in the jaw.

    Yawning, chewing, talking, all easier when the muscles aren’t fighting you.

  • A plan to spot the triggers between sessions.

    You leave with one or two things to notice in daily life so the relief lasts longer than the table.

What a session looks like

  1. Quick intake (about 5 minutes).

    We talk through your symptoms, sleep, stress, dental history, and what you’ve already tried. Intra-oral consent (if it’s relevant) is discussed here, not assumed.

  2. Targeted hands-on work (about 45 to 50 minutes).

    Neck, jaw, temples, and the connected upper-back patterns. External work always, intra-oral only with consent. Pressure adapts to what feels right.

  3. An at-home plan you’ll actually do (about 5 minutes).

    A jaw release technique, a posture cue, a moment in your day to notice if you’re clenching. Small, doable things that compound between sessions.
