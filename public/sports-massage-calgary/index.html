What is sports massage?

Sports massage is hands-on work built around how an active body actually functions. It blends deep tissue, trigger point, and myofascial techniques with range-of-motion work to help you train, recover, and come back from injury. Whether you’re a competitive athlete, a weekend runner, a lifter, or just someone who wants to keep moving without breaking down, the focus is on the specific tissues your sport is loading and what they need to keep doing it.

Benefits of sports massage

  • Faster recovery between training sessions.

    Targeted work on overloaded tissue helps you feel ready sooner without skipping training days.

  • Targeted relief for sport-specific overuse.

    Runners, lifters, cyclists, hockey players, all have their own tight spots. We work with the patterns your sport actually creates.

  • Better range of motion and movement quality.

    When stiff tissue lets go, joints move more freely, which often translates to better performance and less injury risk.

  • Reduced injury risk from imbalances and tight spots.

    Catching restrictions before they become injuries is one of the most underrated reasons to train with regular sports massage.

  • A clear plan that fits your training schedule.

    We work backwards from your races, competitions, or training blocks so sessions are scheduled around your body’s rhythm.

  • Nervous-system support so hard training stays sustainable.

    Recovery isn’t only about the muscles. Calming the system as a whole helps you sleep, train, and adapt.

What a session looks like

  1. Quick intake (about 5 minutes).

    We talk through what you’re training for, what’s bothering you, and what your week ahead looks like. No long forms, just a real conversation.

  2. Targeted hands-on work (about 45 to 50 minutes).

    Sport-specific work on the tissues that are doing the most. Pressure adapts to what your body needs that day, never just push through pain to prove a point.

  3. An at-home plan you’ll actually use (about 5 minutes).

    One or two simple things to take with you. A mobility drill, a stretch to add before a hard session, a warning sign to watch for. Small, doable things that fit your training.
