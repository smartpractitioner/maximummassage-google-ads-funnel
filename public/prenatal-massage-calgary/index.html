Benefits of pregnancy and postpartum massage

  • Less back, hip, and pelvic pain.

    As your centre of gravity shifts, your low back and hips take on extra load. Targeted side-lying work eases that strain.

  • Less swelling and pressure in your hands and feet.

    Sessions can include gentle lymphatic work to help your body move accumulated fluid out of swollen ankles, legs, and hands.

  • Better sleep and calmer nerves.

    Massage activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which lowers cortisol and helps your body actually rest.

  • Mood support, especially in postpartum.

    Research links regular massage to improved serotonin and dopamine levels, and to reduced symptoms of postpartum depression and anxiety.

  • Faster recovery after delivery.

    Specialized postpartum work supports tissue healing, reduces muscle soreness, and addresses changes from c-section or vaginal birth.

  • Time that's actually for you.

    Sometimes the most therapeutic part is one hour where someone is taking care of you instead.

Side-lying with supportive pillows and bolsters. This is the safest and most comfortable position throughout pregnancy because it avoids pressure on the major blood vessels and uterine ligaments. You can lie on either side; many people find left side most comfortable in later trimesters.
